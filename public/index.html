üìä What is RSI (Relative Strength Index)?

RSI measures momentum on a scale of 0-100.

  • Above 70: Overbought - Price may drop soon (consider selling)
  • Below 30: Oversold - Price may rise soon (consider buying)
  • Around 50: Neutral - No strong signal

üí° Beginner Tip: RSI works best in ranging markets. In strong trends, it can stay overbought/oversold for extended periods.

üìà What is MACD?

MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) shows trend direction and momentum.

  • MACD above Signal Line: Bullish (upward momentum)
  • MACD below Signal Line: Bearish (downward momentum)
  • Histogram: Shows strength of the trend

üí° Beginner Tip: Look for crossovers - when MACD crosses above the signal line, it's a buy signal. When it crosses below, it's a sell signal.

üìâ What are Bollinger Bands?

Bollinger Bands show price volatility and potential reversal points.

  • Price at Upper Band: Overbought - may reverse down
  • Price at Lower Band: Oversold - may reverse up
  • Bands Widening: High volatility
  • Bands Narrowing: Low volatility (breakout coming)

üí° Beginner Tip: When bands squeeze together (narrow), a big price move is often coming. Watch for the breakout direction!

üìä What are Moving Averages?

Moving Averages smooth out price data to identify trends.

  • Price above MA: Uptrend
  • Price below MA: Downtrend
  • Golden Cross: Short MA crosses above long MA (bullish)
  • Death Cross: Short MA crosses below long MA (bearish)

üí° Beginner Tip: The 200-day MA is considered the most important. Price above it = long-term uptrend.
